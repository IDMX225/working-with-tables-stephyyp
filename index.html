9 MOST DEVASTATING EARTHQUAKES IN EARTH'S HISTORY

This table lists some of the most devastating earthquakes in history, including their dates, locations, magnitudes, depths, summaries of damage, and number of fatalities.

Earthquake Date & Location Magnitude Depth of Epicenter Summary of Damage Number of Fatalities
Shansi Earthquake (1556) - China Estimated 8.0 Estimated 20-30 km This is the deadliest earthquake on record, occurring in the early Ming Dynasty. It caused widespread destruction across the region, particularly in the province of Shaanxi. Over 830,000 people
Indian Ocean Earthquake (2004) - Coastal regions around Indian Ocean 9.1–9.3 ~30 km A massive undersea earthquake triggered a series of tsunamis that devastated coastal regions in countries like Indonesia, Thailand, Sri Lanka, India, and others around the Indian Ocean. Over 230,000–280,000 people
Tangshan Earthquake (1976) - China 7.5 ~11 km This earthquake occurred near Tangshan, China, and resulted in massive destruction. The densely populated area was particularly affected, and recovery was hindered by poor infrastructure. Approximately 242,000 people
Haiti Earthquake (2010) - Haiti 7.0 ~13 km This earthquake devastated Haiti, causing widespread structural collapse, especially in the capital, Port-au-Prince. It triggered a humanitarian crisis and left millions of people homeless. Around 160,000 people
Great Kanto Earthquake (1923) - Japan 7.9 ~20 km Striking the Kanto region, including Tokyo and Yokohama, this earthquake caused massive destruction, particularly from fires that followed. It was one of the deadliest natural disasters in Japan's history. Over 142,000 people
Nankaido Earthquake (1946) - Japan 8.1 ~10 km Occurring off the coast of Japan, this earthquake caused widespread tsunamis that devastated coastal areas in Japan, as well as in other parts of the Pacific. Over 1,400 people (due to the tsunami)
Lisbon Earthquake (1755) - Portugal Estimated 8.5–9.0 ~30 km One of the deadliest earthquakes in Europe, this disaster also caused tsunamis and fires, destroying much of Lisbon and affecting nearby areas in Portugal, Morocco, and Spain. Around 60,000–100,000 people
Alaska Earthquake (1964) - Alaska 9.2 ~25 km This was the most powerful earthquake ever recorded in North America. It caused widespread damage in Alaska, as well as a massive tsunami that affected coastal areas around the Pacific Ocean. 131 people
Nepal Earthquake (2015) - South Asia 7.8 ~15 km This earthquake devastated Nepal, particularly the capital, Kathmandu, and surrounding areas. It caused significant infrastructure damage and triggered landslides in mountainous regions. Over 9,000 people

Note: The number of fatalities and damage estimates are approximate and can vary based on different sources.

Sources: USGS, National Geographic, various historical records.

Disclaimer: This table is for informational purposes only and does not represent a comprehensive list of all devastating earthquakes.

For more information, please refer to the respective sources.
